How to Start a Kachi Ghani Oil Business in India: Complete Guide

The demand for pure, unadulterated "Kachi Ghani" oil is at an all-time high in India. As consumers move away from refined oils due to health concerns, the opportunity for small and medium-scale Kachi Ghani oil businesses has exploded. This guide walks you through the entire process of setting up your own unit.

What is Kachi Ghani?

Kachi Ghani refers to the traditional method of extracting oil using a "Ghani" (expeller) at very low temperatures. Unlike modern refinery processes that use chemicals and high heat, Kachi Ghani preserves the natural vitamins, minerals, and fatty acids of the seeds.

Step 1: Market Research & Location

Identify the most popular oils in your region. In North India, Mustard Oil dominates, while West India prefers Groundnut Oil and South India relies on Coconut Oil. Choose a location with good ventilation and enough space for raw material storage and machine operation.

Step 2: Selecting the Right Kachi Ghani Machine

Your machine is the heart of your business. A low-quality machine will result in low oil yield and frequent breakdowns. Yamuna Enterprises offers specialized cold-press machines that achieve the authentic Kachi Ghani texture and aroma.

  • 3 HP Models: Best for startups and local retail points.
  • 5 HP Models: Designed for 24/7 commercial production.

Step 3: Investment & Budgeting

Starting a Kachi Ghani unit requires an investment ranging from ₹1.5 Lakhs to ₹5 Lakhs, depending on the scale. This includes:

  • Machine Cost: ₹95,000 - ₹2,00,000
  • Raw Material (Seeds): ₹30,000 - ₹1,00,000
  • Licenses & Packaging: ₹10,000 - ₹25,000

Step 4: Licenses & Regulatory Compliance

To operate legally in India, you will need the following:

  • FSSAI License: Mandatory for all food businesses.
  • GST Registration: Required for tax compliance and bulk sales.
  • Udyam Registration: To avail of government MSME benefits and loans.
  • Trade License: From your local municipal corporation.

Step 5: Sourcing Raw Materials

Quality seeds mean quality oil. Source seeds directly from farmers or wholesale mandis to maximize your profit margins. Ensure the moisture content is low (ideally below 8%) for better oil extraction.
